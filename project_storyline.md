# Project Storyline

## Problem

Sales data can show what happened, but leadership still needs clarity on why revenue is concentrated, where the strongest markets are, and which trends should shape commercial action.

## Approach

Rahul Sehrawat framed the project as a business review engagement: standardize the sales dataset in Python, evaluate category, product, geography, and time-based performance, then convert the results into SQL reporting logic, dashboard planning, and executive recommendations.

## Key Insights

- Revenue is concentrated in a limited set of categories and products.
- Karnataka stands out as the lead market and a benchmark for execution.
- Q2 and May are the strongest performance windows.
- Premium and enterprise orders drive outsized value relative to their order share.
- The September to December slowdown is the most important trend that needs validation before forecasting.

## Business Impact

This project demonstrates how analytics can support revenue planning, category prioritization, distributor review, and regional execution. It also highlights Rahul Sehrawat's transition into analytics by turning operational thinking into structured commercial insight.
