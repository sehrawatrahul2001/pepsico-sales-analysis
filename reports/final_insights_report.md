# Final Insights Report

## Executive Summary

The analysis reviewed **197,430 orders** and **INR 53.01M** in revenue across PepsiCo's beverage portfolio. The business is strong, but performance is clearly concentrated by category, product, market, and order-value tier.

## Key Findings

### 1. Category concentration is high

Carbonated Soft Drinks and Juice together generate the majority of revenue, making them the core commercial engines of the portfolio.

### 2. A small product group drives outsized sales

Top products such as Mountain Dew, Sting Gold Rush, and 7UP are major revenue contributors and should be treated as protected growth assets.

### 3. Karnataka is the strongest market

The state stands out as the lead geography and should be used as a benchmark for commercial execution and market learning.

### 4. Q2 and May are the peak performance windows

The sales pattern aligns with stronger seasonal beverage demand and gives the business a clear planning window for stock and activation focus.

### 5. Premium and enterprise orders carry stronger value

Higher-value orders contribute a disproportionately large share of revenue, showing that mix quality matters as much as order volume.

### 6. Late-year performance needs validation

The sharp September to December slowdown is the most important caution in the dataset and should be validated before it is used for forecasting or target-setting.

## Recommendations

- protect the strongest categories and products through availability and distributor focus
- use leading markets as execution benchmarks for expansion states
- emphasize premium-value mix in commercial planning
- investigate the late-year decline before relying on it in business forecasts
